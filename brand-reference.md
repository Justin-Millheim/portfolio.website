# Justin Millheim — Brand Reference

> Working reference for building justinmillheim.com and keeping every surface consistent. Short on purpose.

---

## Throughline

**Clearer systems, closer teams, better work.**

This is the core line. Use it as the hero tagline, the LinkedIn one-liner, and anywhere the brand needs a single statement. It names what you do (clarity), who it's for (teams/people), and the payoff (quality), in a cadence that reads human instead of like a mission statement.

- **Hero (site):** use the throughline as the tagline under the name. The H1 can expand on it (e.g. "I build clearer systems, closer teams, and better work.").
- **Short slots (footer, social bio):** "Clearer systems, closer teams, better work." already works short. No separate tagline needed.

## Voice

Three rules:

1. **Specific over generic.** Name the real thing (the via ferrata you guided, the 19-contact CRM), not "various leadership experiences." Concrete details are proof.
2. **Confident, not corporate.** Plain and direct. Say what you made and what happened. Skip the synergy/leverage/paradigm vocabulary.
3. **Warm, with a wry edge.** A little dry humor is on-brand. Never stiff, never trying too hard.

### Avoid AI mannerisms (hard rule)

These are the tells. Cut them everywhere (site copy, résumé, outreach):

- **Em dashes:** one per paragraph at most, then use periods, colons, or parentheses.
- **No "it's not X, it's Y"** (and its cousin "not just X, but Y"). It's a dead giveaway.
- **Cut filler and hedges:** "genuinely," "truly," "it's worth noting," "that said," "in today's world."
- **Vary sentence length.** AI writing marches in the same rhythm. Mix short punches with longer lines.
- **No tidy three-part summaries** that exist just to sound complete.

### Don't / Do

- **Don't:** "Results-driven product leader leveraging cross-functional synergies to drive impactful outcomes."
- **Do:** "Builder PM. I turn messy problems into systems people actually want to use."

## Color — Ink & Ember (dark mode only)

| Token | Hex | Role |
|---|---|---|
| Canvas | `#15110D` | Primary background. Warm near-black. |
| Surface | `#211A14` | Cards, panels, raised areas. |
| Deep | `#0F0C08` | Recessed panels (e.g. the build widget). |
| Cream | `#F1E8DC` | Primary text. Does most of the work. |
| Muted | `#9A8C7C` | Secondary text, captions, hints. |
| Line | `#34291D` | Hairline borders and dividers. |
| Ember (primary) | `#E86A33` | CTAs, headline emphasis, links, active states. |
| Brass (accent) | `#C99A52` | Quiet second note: labels, live cues, tags. |

**Ratio rule (this is the brand):** ember is a spark, not a coat of paint. Use it on the one thing that matters most per view. Brass is the supporting warm note. Cream and the dark surfaces carry ~90% of every screen. When in doubt, use less ember.

## Type

- **Fraunces** — display. Headlines, the wordmark, big moments.
- **Hanken Grotesk** — body. Paragraphs, UI copy, descriptions.
- **JetBrains Mono** — labels and meta. Eyebrows, tags, dates, status. Carries the "builder" signal.

Pairing: Fraunces for what people notice, Hanken for what they read, Mono for the small structural labels.

---

## Decisions on record

- **No logo, monogram, or maker's mark.** The wordmark (your name in Fraunces, ember on the surname) is the mark. Do not re-add a logo for the job search. A maker's mark is a fun separate project for the engraved-gifts side, not this.
- **Dark mode is the default and only theme.**
- **Section structure:** Home, Work, Projects (Shipped + Log views), About, plus a `/now` page.
