"AI enthusiast" gets a bad rap, and I get why. It usually means someone reposting screenshots. That is not what I mean by it.

I am all-in on agentic AI because of what it does to the gap between an idea and a working thing. That gap used to be huge. It is collapsing, and I would rather be early and fluent than skeptical and late.

Most of what I build now runs on it. Claude Skills that turn a repeatable task into something I can trigger in a sentence. MCP integrations that let an agent actually reach into my tools instead of just talking about them. Claude Code workflows that take a job from messy idea to shipped in an afternoon. This very site was built that way. At Adobe, my internship is essentially this question pointed at a real team: where does agentic AI actually speed up analyst and PM work, and where is it just noise?

The part that excites me is not the models themselves. It is that the leverage is moving to people who can clearly describe what good looks like. That has always been the PM's job. Now it is also the builder's job, and the line between the two is disappearing.
