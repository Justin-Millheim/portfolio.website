---
title: "What \"Connector\" means to me"
date: "2026-05-27"
excerpt: "The best systems I have built are made of people. Connecting them well is its own kind of product work."
---

I used to think of "people stuff" as the soft part of the job, separate from the real work. I had it backwards. The connecting *is* the work.

As president of the Product Management Association, the thing I am proudest of is not the Notion system I built. It is the partner network behind it: the speakers, alumni, and PM professionals who now show up because someone made the introduction and made it worth their time. As Get Seeded chair at Lassonde, my whole job is to make a room where student founders feel safe enough to pitch and energized enough to come back. That does not happen by accident. It gets designed.

The same instinct shows up everywhere. A peer-mentorship program to pair incoming and current MBA students. A small health-accountability group I started just to keep people consistent. Trips I plan for friends where the real deliverable is everyone feeling like they belong there.

Connecting people is a lot like building a product. You reduce the friction to getting started. You make the value obvious. You design the experience so the slow person does not feel slow and the fast person does not get reckless. And when it works, people think it just happened on its own. That is the goal.

Builder and connector are not two different sides of me. They are the same instinct pointed at two different materials: one at systems, one at people.
