I have a laser engraver and a habit of wanting to give people something personal. The machine was never the hard part. The hard part was the art.

A laser does not want a pretty picture. It wants strict black-and-white line work: no gradients, no gray, no fuzzy edges, clean vectors it can actually cut. Getting there by hand, every time, for every gift, was the thing that kept good ideas from ever making it onto a board or a bottle.

So I built a Claude Skill that owns that whole workflow. You start with a fuzzy idea for a gift, and it walks through it in stages: brainstorm the concept, define the image, generate the actual SVG line art, then refine it. The output is engraver-ready by default, black-and-white vector art with none of the stuff a laser chokes on.

What I like about it is that it pushed me to encode taste, not just steps. The rules for what makes a clean engrave are baked in, so I am not re-explaining "no grayscale, no soft edges" every single time. The skill remembers what good looks like so I can stay focused on the idea and the person I am making it for.
