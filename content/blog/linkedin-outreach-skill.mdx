title: "A skill for outreach that does not sound like a robot"
date: "2026-04-30"
excerpt: "Cold outreach fails when it sounds templated. I built a skill that drafts sharp, personal messages worth replying to."
---

The reason most cold outreach gets ignored is not that people are too busy. It is that they can smell a template from the first line.

I do a fair amount of professional outreach: connection notes, the occasional InMail, asks for informational interviews. Done well, it opens doors. Done lazily, it is noise that makes you look worse than saying nothing. I wanted a way to do it well, consistently, without spending twenty minutes agonizing over every message.

So I built a skill for it. It is not a spam cannon. It drafts genuinely personalized outreach: it works from who the person actually is and why I am reaching out, and it writes a message specific enough that a template could never have produced it. Connection requests, recruiter notes, an informational-interview ask, an invite, each with its own shape, each meant to earn a reply rather than just land in an inbox.

The thing I am most careful about is that it sharpens my voice instead of replacing it. The goal was never to automate relationships. It was to remove the friction that kept me from reaching out at all, so the good messages actually get sent.

Outreach is just connecting with extra steps. Take the friction out, and you do more of the thing that actually matters.
