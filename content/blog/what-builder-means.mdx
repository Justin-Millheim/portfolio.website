---
title: "What \"Builder\" means to me"
date: "2026-05-28"
excerpt: "Builder is not a job title for me. It is the instinct to take a messy, undefined thing and turn it into a system that works."
---

People hear "builder" and picture someone writing code. I do some of that, but it is not what the word means to me.

For me, building is the instinct to look at a vague, messy thing and not be able to leave it alone until it is a system that works. A new-product process that kept stalling. A student club whose knowledge lived in scattered docs. A weekend trip with friends that could have been chaos. A slab of walnut on a laser bed. Different materials, same instinct: find the shape hiding in the mess and give it structure.

The thread through every PM role I have had is the same. At Domo it was a next-gen ETL engine. At Visier it was the ingestion pipeline that decided how fast customers reached value. At Mity it was a stage-gate process across five teams. None of those were really about the feature. They were about building the system around the feature so the work could repeat without me holding it together by hand.

That is the part I care about most: building things that outlast the moment. A tool that still works when I am not in the room. A process the next person can pick up without a single "wait, how did the last person do this?" conversation.
