---
title: "A songwriting skill, from concept to lyrics"
date: "2026-05-05"
excerpt: "Most AI lyric tools hand you a finished blob you cannot fix. I wanted a collaborator that helps me write, then helps me ship."
---

I like writing songs, and I had tried the usual AI lyric generators. They all have the same problem: you describe a vibe, they hand you a finished block of words, and if it is almost-right there is nowhere to go. No collaboration, no revision, just take-it-or-leave-it.

So I built a songwriting skill that works the way actual writing works: in passes.

It starts as a facilitator, not a vending machine. It helps me find the concept before it writes a single line, then drafts structure, verses, a chorus, a bridge. The part I cared most about is a dedicated refinement phase, where the song is treated as a draft to improve rather than a final answer. And at the end it can output a production prompt, the style description you feed an AI music tool to actually hear the thing.

The design lesson here is the same one I keep relearning: the value is not in generating text, it is in the workflow around it. A good chorus is a few revisions away from a mediocre one, and a tool that refuses to revise is just a fancier blank page.
