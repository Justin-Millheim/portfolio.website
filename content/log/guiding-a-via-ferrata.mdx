Two days near Vernal. Cowboy camping, fishing, and a via ferrata route I led a small group through. A via ferrata is a climbing path with fixed cables and rungs, so the risk is real but managed. You are exposed, but you are clipped in.

That phrase stuck with me on the drive home: exposed, but clipped in.

## The overlap with product

Guiding a group up a cliff is the same job as taking a team through a roadmap. You set the route. You make the risk legible so nobody freezes. You keep the slow person from feeling slow and the fast person from getting reckless. And you bring everyone over the top feeling like they did it themselves.

The cliff just makes the stakes obvious. At work the exposure is quieter, but the muscle is the same.

## Why I plan trips like products

Good experiences do not happen by accident. They get designed. A little structure, the right pacing, and a clear payoff at the end. Whether it is a weekend or a launch, that is the same work I like doing.
